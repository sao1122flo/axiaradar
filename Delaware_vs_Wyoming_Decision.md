## What matters for your specific case

### Pulls toward **Delaware**
- **Flo's market is LatAm fintech and businesses buying market intelligence.** Banks and enterprise customers across Latin America default-trust "Delaware LLC" — it's the entity type they see most often from US counterparties.
- **Two consumer-/business-facing brands.** Each will collect payments, sign contracts, possibly integrate with payment aggregators. Higher legibility = fewer "what's a Wyoming LLC?" friction moments.
- **One-and-done choice.** If either business ever gets acquired or you decide to raise, DE removes a step.
- **Cost delta is small in absolute terms.** ~$240/yr extra is the price of a couple dinners; not material at the scale where this matters.

### Pulls toward **Wyoming**
- **Pure cost arithmetic.** ~$2,400 saved over a decade.
- **Privacy.** Slightly less public-record exposure.
- **You're a deliberate bootstrapper.** If you genuinely never plan to raise or sell, the DE premium is paying for optionality you won't use.

### Doesn't actually matter for you
- Court of Chancery (you're not litigating)
- VC-readiness (you're not raising)
- Operating Agreement flexibility (both are essentially unlimited)
- Series LLC (you don't need it)
- Tax structure (identical at federal level; both have $0 state income tax)

---

## Recommendation

**Delaware.**

The cost gap ($240/yr) is real but small. The legibility gap is large and compounds: every bank application, every vendor onboarding, every partner due-diligence pass goes faster with a Delaware LLC. For a holding company that exists to support two operating businesses serving cross-border markets, that legibility is worth the premium.

Wyoming would be the right answer if either business were:
- Pure SaaS to US-only customers
- Run as a side hobby with minimal external interactions
- Likely to never bank with anyone other than Mercury

That's not Cordillera Ventures.
